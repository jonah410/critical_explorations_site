Philosophy of Mind – Introductory

  • Consciousness: An Introduction – Susan Blackmore
    A comprehensive and accessible introduction to the science and philosophy of consciousness.
  • The Mind’s I – Douglas Hofstadter & Daniel Dennett
    A collection of essays and stories exploring self, mind, and consciousness.
  • Philosophy of Mind: A Beginner’s Guide – Ian Ravenscroft
    A clear and concise introduction to the main questions and theories in philosophy of mind.
  • Mind: A Brief Introduction – John Searle
    Searle’s accessible overview of the philosophy of mind, consciousness, and intentionality.
  • Mind: Introduction to Cognitive Science – Paul Thagard
    An approachable introduction to cognitive science and the study of mind.

← Back to Philosophy of Mind

``` ```html:reading-list/philosophy-of-mind/intermediate.html

Philosophy of Mind – Intermediate

  • Self Comes to Mind – Antonio Damasio
    A neuroscientist’s account of how consciousness and self arise in the brain.
  • How to Create a Mind – Ray Kurzweil
    Kurzweil explores the workings of the human brain and how to replicate it in machines.
  • Philosophy of Mind – Jaegwon Kim
    A widely used textbook covering the main issues in philosophy of mind.
  • The Conscious Brain – David Rosenthal
    A philosophical exploration of consciousness and higher-order thought.

← Back to Philosophy of Mind

``` ```html:reading-list/philosophy-of-mind/advanced.html

Philosophy of Mind – Advanced

  • Consciousness Explained – Daniel Dennett
    Dennett’s influential theory of consciousness and the “multiple drafts” model.
  • The Conscious Mind – David Chalmers
    A rigorous argument for the “hard problem” of consciousness and property dualism.
  • Being No One – Thomas Metzinger
    A deep dive into the philosophy and neuroscience of selfhood and consciousness.
  • Mind in Life – Evan Thompson
    An enactive approach to mind, consciousness, and biology.

Artificial Intelligence: Technical – Introductory

  • Artificial Intelligence: A Guide for Thinking Humans – Melanie Mitchell
    A clear, non-technical introduction to the field of AI, its history, and its future.
  • Deep Learning Illustrated – Jon Krohn, Grant Beyleveld, & Aglaé Bassens
    A visual, intuitive introduction to deep learning concepts and applications.
  • Make Your Own Neural Network – Tariq Rashid
    A hands-on beginner’s guide to building neural networks from scratch.
  • Artificial Intelligence: A Very Short Introduction – Margaret Boden
    A concise and insightful overview of AI by a pioneer in the field.

← Back to AI: Technical

``` ```html:reading-list/ai-technical/intermediate.html

Artificial Intelligence: Technical – Intermediate

  • Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning – Christopher Bishop
    A widely used textbook on statistical techniques in machine learning and pattern recognition.
  • Deep Learning – Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, & Aaron Courville
    The definitive textbook on deep learning, covering theory and practical applications.
  • Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction – Richard Sutton & Andrew Barto
    The classic introduction to reinforcement learning, from foundational concepts to advanced topics.
  • Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction – Kevin Murphy
    A modern introduction to probabilistic approaches in machine learning.

← Back to AI: Technical

``` ```html:reading-list/ai-technical/advanced.html

Artificial Intelligence: Technical – Advanced

  • Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach – Stuart Russell & Peter Norvig
    The standard comprehensive textbook for advanced AI students and practitioners.
  • Probabilistic Graphical Models: Principles and Techniques – Daphne Koller & Nir Friedman
    A deep dive into graphical models and their applications in AI.
  • Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms – David MacKay
    A mathematically rich text connecting information theory and machine learning.
